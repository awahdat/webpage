Ahmad Zia Wahdat

Postdoctoral Research Associate, Center for Food Demand Analysis & Sustainability, Purdue University

Fields: Food & Agricultural Economics, Behavioral & Experimental Economics, Household Finance

Ph.D. in Agricultural Economics, Purdue University

Ph.D. Minor Specialization: Computational Science

My research is in the area of applied microeconomics. I focus on food and agricultural economics, behavioral and experimental economics, and household finance topics. I study consumer behavior, household financial decisions, and attitudes in the event of economic shocks or natural disasters. I also study the vulnerability of food and agribusinesses, i.e., resiliency against disasters and market shocks, investment and risk-taking capacity after natural disasters.

Since my work requires advanced data methods and data analysis tools, I specialized in computational science during my doctoral program. Since 2010, I have worked with a range of datasets: cross-sectional, panel, national surveys, market surveys, and randomized control trials both in the field and lab. I have worked with various data analysis software: Stata, R, Python, Matlab, GAMS, ArcGIS.

Before my Ph.D., I worked with organizations in the non-profit and academic sectors. Most of my work involved academic research or project management. I really enjoyed my time at the World Bank, Harvard Business School, MIT Sloan School of Management, German International Cooperation, and CSIS. In my free time (if available!) I enjoy cooking, reading, improving my coding skills, and thinking of solutions for disadvantaged units in a society.
